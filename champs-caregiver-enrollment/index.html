Enrollment Guide

CHAMPS enrollment: how caregivers actually get paid

CHAMPS — the Community Health Automated Medicaid Processing System — is the State of Michigan's Medicaid provider system. Every paid Home Help caregiver must be enrolled in it before a single hour can be paid. Here's the process without the confusion.

Call ACAI Today
1Before a single hour is paid

Why CHAMPS matters

Michigan pays Home Help providers through its Medicaid infrastructure. That means the caregiver — even a daughter caring for her own mother — becomes an enrolled individual provider with a record in CHAMPS. MDHHS states that providers must be enrolled in CHAMPS and pass a criminal history screen before payment. No enrollment, no payment — and enrollment is not retroactive pay for earlier care.

2Four steps, in order

What enrollment involves

  1. 1

    Gather your information

    Social Security number, date of birth, contact details, and basic identity information for the caregiver.

  2. 2

    Register in CHAMPS

    Create the online provider account and complete the individual provider application through Michigan's MILogin / CHAMPS portal.

  3. 3

    Pass the screening

    A criminal history screen is required before a provider can be paid.

  4. 4

    Connect to the client's case

    The enrolled provider is linked to the Home Help client's authorized services so hours can be reported and paid.

3Mistakes that delay caregiver pay

The delays we see most

  • Name or SSN mismatches between the application and official records.
  • Unfinished applications — started online, never submitted, and nobody noticed.
  • Waiting to enroll until after the assessment, adding weeks to the first payment.
  • Missed follow-ups when the state requests a correction or more information.

ACAI's role: we help families understand each enrollment step, know what documents to have ready, and keep the process moving — at no cost. The State of Michigan runs CHAMPS and makes all enrollment decisions.

How long does CHAMPS enrollment take?

It varies — a clean, complete application moves much faster than one with mismatched information. Starting enrollment early, alongside the Home Help assessment, is the best way to shorten time-to-first-payment.

Does a caregiver need a business or tax ID?

Individual Home Help providers typically enroll under their own Social Security number. Ask us about your situation and we will explain what applies.

What shows up in the criminal history screen?

The state reviews criminal history before approving a provider for payment. A record does not automatically disqualify someone in every case — the state makes the determination.

Can ACAI enroll me in CHAMPS?

CHAMPS enrollment is the caregiver's own state application, but ACAI walks families through what each step means, what to prepare, and what to expect — free of charge.
