Eligibility Guide

Home Help eligibility, in plain language

Three things drive Home Help eligibility: Medicaid, documented daily care needs, and an MDHHS assessment. Here's what each one means for your family — and the caregiver rules that surprise people most often.

1Where eligibility starts

Medicaid comes first

Home Help is a Michigan Medicaid benefit, so the person receiving care needs active Michigan Medicaid. Not sure about status? That's a normal starting point — we help families sort it out every week.

Care rooted in trust

Eligibility is the first step toward the kind of care your family already gives, now recognized and supported.

2What counts as daily care

Daily care needs: ADLs and IADLs

Eligibility is built around needing hands-on help with daily activities:

  • ADLs — Activities of Daily Living

    Bathing, dressing, grooming, eating, toileting, transferring (getting in and out of bed or a chair), and mobility around the home.

  • IADLs — Instrumental Activities

    Meal preparation, laundry, light housekeeping, and shopping connected to the person's care needs.

The person's doctor certifies the need for personal care, and the specific mix of tasks — and the monthly hours for each — is determined in the assessment.

3Who decides, and how

The MDHHS assessment

An MDHHS adult services worker meets with the person (usually at home) to review what help they genuinely need day to day. That assessment produces the authorized task list and monthly hours. Being prepared — honest, specific, and organized about daily struggles — makes a real difference, and it's one of the places ACAI's free preparation guidance helps most.

4What surprises families most

Caregiver rules families should know

  • Family can usually be paid — adult children, relatives, friends, and neighbors commonly enroll as providers.
  • Spouses have special rules under MDHHS policy — ask us before assuming either way.
  • Enrollment comes before payment — CHAMPS registration and a criminal history screen are required first.
  • Hours are authorized, not unlimited — payment covers the assessed, approved hours only.

Does income affect Home Help eligibility?

Home Help runs through Medicaid, which has its own financial rules. If the person already has active Michigan Medicaid, the financial piece is largely settled; if not, Medicaid eligibility becomes the first step.

Can someone with dementia qualify?

A dementia diagnosis alone does not decide it — what matters is the hands-on help the person needs with daily activities. Many people living with dementia qualify based on their assessed ADL and IADL needs.

What if the assessment authorizes fewer hours than we need?

The authorization reflects the documented need. If circumstances change or needs increase, a reassessment can be requested. We help families understand what to document and how to ask.

Can more than one caregiver be enrolled?

In some situations more than one provider can share authorized hours. Ask us about your family's setup and we will explain how MDHHS handles it.
