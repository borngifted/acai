Paid Family Caregiving

Get paid for the care you are already providing

If you help a parent, relative, friend, or neighbor with bathing, dressing, meals, or daily life in Michigan, the Home Help Program may pay you for that care. Here is exactly how it works — and how to find out if your family qualifies.

Call ACAI Today
1Four things to know

The short version

  • The person receiving care generally needs active Michigan Medicaid and hands-on help with daily activities.
  • MDHHS assesses their needs and authorizes monthly hours of personal care.
  • You enroll as an individual Home Help provider — including CHAMPS registration and a criminal history screen.
  • Once approved, you are paid for authorized hours at your county's rate.

2Choosing the caregiver

Who can be paid

Adult daughters and sons, other relatives, friends, neighbors, and existing caregivers are the most common paid providers. Some relationships have special rules under MDHHS policy — spouses, for example, are treated differently — so the smartest first step is a quick conversation about your specific situation.

3Rates, hours & honest expectations

How much can caregivers be paid?

Caregiver payment varies by county, eligibility, authorized hours, enrollment status, and MDHHS approval. MDHHS bases the hourly rate on the county where the client lives. Because both the rate and the authorized hours differ family to family, honest guidance beats any number you see advertised — ask us what a realistic picture looks like for your county.

Be careful with big promises. No company can guarantee approval, hours, or a pay amount. Final decisions are always made by MDHHS. What ACAI can do is make sure your application and enrollment are set up right the first time.

5Straight answers

Common questions

See all FAQs →

Can I be paid for care I am already giving for free?

Possibly, going forward — Home Help pays for authorized care after approval and enrollment; it does not pay retroactively for past unpaid care. The sooner the process starts, the sooner payment can begin.

Do I need special training or a license?

Individual Home Help providers generally do not need a professional license. You do need to complete provider enrollment, register in CHAMPS, and pass a criminal history screen before payment.

Can I work another job and still be a paid caregiver?

Often yes — authorized care hours simply need to be actually provided and documented under program rules. We can help you think through a realistic schedule.

What if my loved one does not have Medicaid yet?

Medicaid is the doorway to Home Help. If your loved one may qualify but is not enrolled, that becomes step one — ask us and we will point you in the right direction.
