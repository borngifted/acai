The Complete Guide

The Michigan Home Help Program, explained for families

Home Help is Michigan's Medicaid-funded personal care program. It helps eligible adults get hands-on assistance with everyday activities at home — and in many situations, the person providing that care can be a relative, friend, or neighbor who is paid for the work they are already doing.

1Two categories of support

What Home Help covers

Home Help, administered by the Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS), provides personal care assistance for people who need hands-on help with daily activities so they can keep living safely at home. Support is organized around two categories:

  • Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) — bathing, dressing, grooming, eating, toileting, transferring, and mobility.
  • Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADLs) — meal preparation, laundry, light housekeeping, shopping, and similar household support connected to the person's care.
2Eligibility, in plain language

Who can receive Home Help

In general, a person may qualify when they have active Michigan Medicaid, need hands-on help with daily activities, and an MDHHS adult services worker assesses and approves that need. The assessment determines which tasks are covered and how many hours are authorized each month.

Good to know: the person's doctor confirms the medical need for personal care, and MDHHS — not any private company — makes the final eligibility and hours decisions.

3Choosing who provides care

Who can be the paid caregiver

Families often choose someone the person already knows and trusts: an adult daughter or son, another relative, a friend, a neighbor, or a current caregiver. To be paid, that person enrolls as an individual Home Help provider, which includes registering in Michigan's CHAMPS system and passing a criminal history screen. Some relationships, such as spouses, have special rules under MDHHS policy — ask us what applies to your family.

4Getting paid for the care you provide

How caregiver payment works

Caregiver payment varies by county, eligibility, authorized hours, enrollment status, and MDHHS approval. MDHHS bases the provider's hourly rate on the county where the client lives, and payment cannot begin until the provider's enrollment is complete and services are authorized.

5Four steps, start to finish

How to get started

  1. 1

    Check your situation

    Confirm Medicaid status, daily care needs, and who would provide the care. Our free eligibility check takes about 60 seconds.

  2. 2

    Request Home Help through MDHHS

    An adult services worker assesses the person's needs at home and determines covered tasks and monthly hours.

  3. 3

    Enroll the caregiver

    The chosen caregiver completes provider enrollment, including CHAMPS registration and the required screenings.

  4. 4

    Care and payment begin

    Once approved and authorized, care is delivered and documented under program rules, and the provider is paid for authorized hours.

ACAI Home Help walks Michigan families through each of these steps at no cost — explaining eligibility, helping organize paperwork, and following up so nobody is left figuring it out alone.
