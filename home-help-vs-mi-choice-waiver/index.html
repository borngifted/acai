Home Help vs. MI Choice Waiver: which fits your family?

These two Michigan programs are constantly confused — both are Medicaid-funded and both help people stay at home, but they cover different needs, run through different doors, and follow different rules. Here's the clean comparison.

What it isMedicaid personal care program for help with daily activities (ADLs/IADLs) at home.Medicaid waiver providing a broader package of home- and community-based services as an alternative to nursing home care.

Typical servicesPersonal care: bathing, dressing, meals, housekeeping, and similar support.Care coordination plus services like personal care, adult day programs, home modifications, transportation, and respite.
Family caregiversA relative, friend, or neighbor can often enroll and be paid as the provider.Some services can involve chosen caregivers, depending on the waiver agency and plan.
2Matching the program to the need

How to think about the choice

  • Needs help with daily activities, wants a trusted person paid to provide it? Home Help is usually the conversation to start with.
  • Needs are closer to nursing-home level, or the family needs a coordinated package (day programs, respite, equipment, transportation)? MI Choice deserves a serious look.
  • Some families use different programs over time as needs change — the right answer today may evolve.

Local note: ACAI's Adult Day Care in Genesee County operates through Active Adult Day Health Center, a Medicaid Waiver provider through the Area Agency on Aging since 1999 — so we know both sides of this comparison firsthand.

Can someone be on both Home Help and MI Choice?

The programs have rules against duplicating the same service, and the right combination depends on the person's situation. The waiver agency and MDHHS determine what is allowed — we help you ask the right questions.

Which program pays family caregivers more?

They are structured differently — Home Help pays enrolled individual providers county-based hourly rates for authorized hours, while MI Choice services are arranged through the waiver agency. There is no universal “pays more” — it depends on the assessed needs and plan.

Who do I call for MI Choice?

MI Choice runs through regional waiver agencies, commonly your Area Agency on Aging. If you are in Genesee County or anywhere in Michigan, call us first and we will point you to the right door for your situation.
