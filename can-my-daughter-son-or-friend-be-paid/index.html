Family Questions

“Can my daughter be paid to take care of me?”

It's the question Michigan families ask most — about daughters, sons, grandkids, friends, and neighbors. For many families the answer is yes, through the Home Help Program. Here are the straight answers, relationship by relationship.

1Five common situations

Relationship by relationship

  1. ✓

    Daughter or son

    Yes, commonly — adult children are among the most frequent paid Home Help providers, once enrolled in CHAMPS and screened.

  2. ✓

    Other relatives

    Grandchildren, siblings, nieces and nephews, and in-laws can generally enroll as providers when program requirements are met.

  3. ✓

    Friends & neighbors

    Yes — Home Help does not require a family relationship. A trusted friend or neighbor already helping out is a natural fit.

  4. !

    Spouses

    Special rules apply to spouses under MDHHS policy — do not assume either way. Ask us and we will explain what applies to your situation.

  5. !

    Legal guardians

    Guardianship and other legal-responsibility relationships also carry special rules. A quick conversation sorts this out fast.

Family, at the center

The people your loved one already trusts are often the best ones to care for them.

2Before payment can start

What every paid caregiver must do

  • Enroll as an individual Home Help provider, including CHAMPS registration.
  • Pass a criminal history screen before payment.
  • Provide and document care within the authorized hours MDHHS approves.
3The other half of the equation

What the person receiving care needs

Active Michigan Medicaid, a documented need for hands-on help with daily activities, and an MDHHS assessment that authorizes services and hours. If those pieces are in place — or could be — the family conversation about who provides the care gets much easier.

The honest caveat: approval is never automatic, and no one can promise payment. What we can promise is a clear explanation of your family's path, at no cost, before you commit to anything.

Can my caregiver live with me?

Yes — live-in family members and caregivers commonly serve as Home Help providers. What matters is the authorized care being provided and documented, not the address.

Can two of my children split the caregiving?

In some situations authorized hours can be shared between enrolled providers. Ask us about your family's arrangement and we will explain how MDHHS handles it.

My mom already pays a private caregiver. Can that person be paid by Home Help instead?

Often yes — an existing private caregiver can typically enroll as her Home Help provider if requirements are met, shifting some or all of the cost off the family.

Does the caregiver have to be a Michigan resident?

The caregiver needs to be able to provide the authorized care in the client's Michigan home and complete Michigan provider enrollment. Ask us about any specific residency questions.
